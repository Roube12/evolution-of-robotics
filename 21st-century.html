21st Century Robotics: Intelligence and Autonomy

The 21st century has seen rapid advances in robotics, characterized by the rise of collaborative robots (cobots), sophisticated humanoid and social robots, groundbreaking medical and service robots, and the integration of AI technologies enabling higher autonomy.

A. Collaborative Robots (Cobots)

Cobots are designed to safely work alongside humans without the need for traditional safety cages. Universal Robots’ UR5 (2008) made automation accessible to small enterprises with its easy programmability and safe human interaction features.

B. Humanoid and Social Robots

Robots like Honda's ASIMO and SoftBank’s Pepper brought social interaction to robotics. ASIMO showcased mobility and environmental awareness, while Pepper specialized in reading human emotions and engaging in conversation.

C. Medical and Service Robots

Robotic systems like the Da Vinci Surgical System (2000) revolutionized surgery with high precision. Meanwhile, consumer robots such as Roomba became common in homes for cleaning automation.

D. AI-Powered Autonomy

Today’s robots feature advanced AI, including machine learning, vision systems, and speech recognition, allowing them to perform tasks in dynamic, unstructured environments. This autonomy underpins innovations from warehouse automation to self-driving delivery robots and personal assistants.
