Growth of Industrial and Research Robotics

1960s–1990s

The period from the 1960s through the 1990s was crucial in the development of modern robotics, marked by breakthroughs in robotic manipulation, artificial intelligence (AI), space exploration, and cultural integration.

A. Robotic Arms and AI Integration

Stanford Arm (1969): Designed by Victor Scheinman, it was the first small, electric robotic arm with six degrees of freedom. It allowed precise, computer-controlled manipulation and laid the foundation for robots like the PUMA.

Shakey the Robot (1966–1972): Created by SRI, Shakey was the first AI-integrated mobile robot capable of perception, planning, and basic decision-making. It featured a TV camera for vision and sensors for navigation.

B. Space Robotics

Canadarm (1981): Built by Canada for NASA, the Canadarm was first used on the Space Shuttle Discovery. It enabled remote space operations such as satellite capture and repair, becoming an iconic example of robotic application in extreme environments.

C. Influence of Pop Culture

Popular media during this period brought robots into the mainstream imagination:

  • Star Wars (1977–): Friendly robots like R2-D2 and C-3PO.
  • Blade Runner (1982): Explored the ethics of AI and humanoid robots.
  • The Terminator (1984–): Showcased powerful autonomous machines and AI threats.

These portrayals influenced how society viewed robots and inspired future scientists, engineers, and roboticists.
